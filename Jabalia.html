What Are Panic Attacks

A panic attack causes sudden, temporary feelings
of fear and strong physical reactions in response
to ordinary, nonthreatening situations. When
you’re having a panic attack, you may sweat a
lot, have difficulty breathing and feel like your
heart’s racing. It may feel like you’re
having a heart attack.

How Long Can A Panic Attack last?

Panic attacks usually last 5 to 20 minutes.
But some people have reported attacks lasting up to an hour.

Sign and Symptoms of a Painic Attacks:

  1. pounding or racing heartbeat.
  2. feeling faint, dizzy or light-headed.
  3. feeling very hot or very cold.
  4. sweating, trembling or shaking.

Reasons (causes) for a panic Attacks

Stress

High levels of stress from work,relationships, or life changes.

Anxiety disorders

Generalized anxiety,social anxiety, or other related conditions.

Trauma

Past traumatic experiences or PTSD.

Phobias

Being exposed to a specific fear (like flying, heights, etc.).

Substance use

Caffeine, drugs, or alcohol can trigger panic attacks.
