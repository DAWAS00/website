Our Story

The primary objective of this project is to explore and address the psychological impact
of war-related sounds, such as bombings and airstrikes, on children living in Gaza. Specifically, the project aims to raise awareness about the increasing cases of panic
attacks among children as a direct result of constant exposure to traumatic war noises.
By highlighting the urgent need for psychological support and intervention, this project
seeks to advocate for the mental well-being of Gaza’s children and to develop coping strategies that can help reduce their anxiety and fear during times of conflict.

Our Team

We are a passionate team of three university students Leen And Bayan
studying Computer Science and Dana study Software Engineering.
United by our dedication to supporting the children of Gaza, we created
this platform to raise awareness and provide guidance about panic attacks.
Through technology and empathy, we aim to make a positive difference in young lives.

The Goals Of The Website

  1. Provide psychological support to children in Gaza through expert collaboration.
  2. Develop the website into a platform with virtual therapy and educational tools.
  3. Create a safe space for children to express their feelings.
  4. Raise awareness about mental health in war-affected areas.
  5. Help children build long-term emotional strength and resilience.
